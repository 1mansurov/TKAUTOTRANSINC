TK Auto Trans Inc. - Terms & Conditions

Carrier and Definitions

TK Auto Trans: ‘TK Auto Trans,’ ‘TK,’ ‘we,’ ‘us,’ or ‘our’ refers to TK Auto Trans Inc., which is fully licensed and bonded, meeting all necessary requirements mandated by the USDOT (MC 820793) and fully compliant with the Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA).

Carrier: TK Auto Trans Inc. and any of its agents, employees, or subcontractors involved in the transport of the vehicle(s).

Shipper: The individual or entity placing the order for the transportation of the vehicle(s). The Shipper is also the customer in this Agreement.

Consignee: The person or entity receiving the vehicle(s) at the destination.

Bill of Lading (BOL): A legal document issued by the Carrier acknowledging the receipt and condition of the vehicle(s) at the time of pickup and delivery.

Inoperable Vehicle: A vehicle that cannot be driven on or off the transport truck under its own power.

Oversized Vehicle: A vehicle that has modifications or dimensions exceeding standard specifications, such as dual wheels, extra-large racks, or lift kits.

Door-to-Door Service: A service where the Carrier will attempt to get as close as legally and safely possible to the stated address for pickup and delivery.

Shipper Responsibilities

Accurate Information: The Shipper must provide accurate pickup and drop-off locations.

Vehicle Preparation: The Shipper is responsible for preparing the vehicle(s) for shipping, including securing loose parts, removing or deactivating toll passes, and ensuring the vehicle has no more than a half tank of fuel (1/4 tank preferred). All personal belongings should be removed unless prior arrangements are made.

Inspection: The Shipper and Consignee must perform a thorough inspection of the vehicle(s) before and after delivery and carefully review the Bill of Lading before signing.

Compliance: The Shipper warrants compliance with all applicable laws, rules, and regulations.

Cleaning: The Shipper is responsible for cleaning the exterior of the vehicle(s) to allow for proper inspection. Skipping this step may impact the initial inspection and could nullify any future damage claims.

Vehicle Ownership and Authorization

Ownership Confirmation: The Shipper confirms they are the legal owner of the vehicle(s) being transported or have been duly authorized by the legal owner to enter into this Agreement.

Authorization: By signing this Agreement, either physically or electronically, the Shipper authorizes TK Auto Trans Inc. (Carrier) and its agents to operate and transport the vehicle(s) from the specified origin to the specified destination by any mode.

Delivery Requirements

The Consignee must be present at the scheduled delivery point at the specified time. If not, a $100 per day storage fee could apply. The Carrier is not liable for non-use charges, such as auto rentals, due to delays or damages.

Payment for transport is due at or prior to delivery without exceptions, unless other arrangements are made beforehand.
